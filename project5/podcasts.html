10 Podcasts to Have on Your Radar

Should you ever find yourself wondering about what to do next on your morning commute, long road trip, or simply your next walk to class, below are a list of top podcasts spanning multiple genres for you to dive into.

  1. Joe Rogan

    Recently involved in controversy about misinformation in the media, it would be remiss to not mention a continuous top contender among the Spotify podcast charts. Hosted by comedian Joe Rogan since 2009, this podcast includes conversations from actors, artists, musicians, and more.


  2. Crime Junkie

    If you are a true crime fanatic, turn no further than to this popular podcast, hosted by Ashley Flowers, and released in 2017. With over 500 million downloads, this podcast doesn’t seem to be going anywhere soon. Get your true crime fix and listen here.


  3. Anything Goes With Emma Chamberlain

    Chamberlain, coming into her fame at a young age on YouTube, started her lifestyle podcast February of 2020. Chamberlain dives into everything and anything, from relationships, to books, to career advice, and to her own personal life experiences.


  4. Up First

    With quick episodes, around 15-20 minutes a day, NPR Up First gives listeners quick updates into what is going on in the world. Keep up to date with the three biggest news stories according to NPR. Launched in April of 2017, Up First is one of the news podcasts giving you what you should know within a brief time.


  5. Call Her Daddy

    Extremely popular with college women, Call Her Daddy has been around since 2018. With a separation of hosts back in 2020, Alexandra Cooper now runs the show solo, giving out advice about love and relationships along the way. It is now exclusively on Spotify.


  6. Stuff You Should Know

    With over 1,400 episodes, Stuff You Should Know is informative, and perfect for the curious mind with burning questions. If you ever wondered about Harry Houdini, how ants work, or John Lennon’s run in with the FBI, take a crack at some of these podcasts’ episodes.


  7. The Bill Simmons Podcast

    For all you sports lovers out there, Bill Simmon’s podcast breaks down all things sports with special guests each episode. Recent episodes recap the Super Bowl, the Harden-Simmons NBA trade, and other sports-related news.


  8. Serial

    With three seasons, each investigating one true story over multiple episodes, Serial exploded on to the podcast season in 2014. Hosted by Sarah Koenig, Serial brings investigative journalism into the audio sphere. With varying lengths of episodes, and winner of the Peabody Award, this podcast is sure to keep you hooked.


  9. Song Exploder

    For artists, musicians, and just fans of music, Hrishikesh Hirway’s music podcasts delves into specific songs and the stories behind them. If you ever have wondered just how your favorite tune continues to stay stuck in your head, or how someone came up with that beat or lyric, this one is for you.


  10. Unlocking Us with Brené Brown

    Sometimes it is hard to have open, honest, conversations with those around you, or even with yourself. Host Brené Brown discusses the human experience: the good, the bad, the confusing. Alongside research, Brown decodes life experiences, gives advice, and starts some emotional conversations.


If you are new to the podcasting world, or stick to one genre, keep these mentioned above in mind. You never know what new thing you might learn or take away from episodes if you expand your horizons of what is out there.
